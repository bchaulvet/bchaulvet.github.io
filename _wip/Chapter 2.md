It investigates the mechanisms by which import dependence on strategic resources (e.g., rare earths, lithium, copper) influences power asymmetries, alliance formations, and conflict dynamics between nations. By analyzing empirical case studies and theoretical frameworks, the paper aims to uncover how CRM trade transitions from economic exchange to geopolitical leverage, ultimately reshaping global power structures and security architectures.
